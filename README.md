# Aprende Jugando — Life Skills Video Game

A school social impact project designed to teach real-life decision-making skills through interactive gameplay.

---

## Overview

*Aprende Jugando* ("Learn by Playing") is an educational video game built in Roblox that places players in everyday situations requiring meaningful choices. Rather than delivering information through lectures or static content, the game lets players experience the consequences of their decisions in a safe, engaging environment.

The premise is simple: young people already spend hours in front of screens. This project redirects that time toward developing skills that traditional education rarely addresses directly.

---

## Problem Statement

Adolescents regularly face situations involving peer pressure, financial decisions, emotional conflict, and stress — yet few school curricula provide practical tools to navigate them. Available resources tend to be either inaccessible or presented in formats that fail to engage young audiences.

This project treats that gap as a design problem.

---

## How It Works

The player controls a young character navigating a series of realistic scenarios:

- A friend pressures them into spending money they don't have
- Someone they know tries to get them to do something they're uncomfortable with
- A conflict arises with another person — ignore it, confront it, or ask for help?

Each decision carries consequences reflected in three in-game metrics: **wellbeing**, **finances**, and **relationships**. There are no lectures. The learning emerges from play.

---

## Skills Addressed

- Financial literacy — budgeting, saving, avoiding unnecessary debt
- Emotional intelligence — managing anger, sadness, and pressure
- Conflict resolution — assertive communication and empathy
- Resistance to peer pressure — saying no without damaging relationships
- Decision-making — thinking before acting

---

## Why a Video Game

Research on game-based learning consistently shows higher retention rates compared to traditional instruction formats. Games engage the brain's memory and decision-making systems actively rather than passively. More practically: it's a format students already use and enjoy.

---

## Development Plan

| Phase | Activity |
|-------|----------|
| Week 1 | Surveys to identify the most common situations young people face |
| Week 2 | Scriptwriting — characters, scenarios, and decision trees |
| Week 3 | Game development in Scratch |
| Week 4 | Peer testing and feedback |
| Week 5 | School-wide presentation and distribution |

## Resources

- **Platform:** [Roblox Studio](https://www.roblox.com/create) — free development environment using Luau scripting
- **Budget:** None — the project relies entirely on time, creativity, and peer collaboration
- **Tools:** Surveys, Scratch editor, feedback sessions

## Goals

- Reach at least 100 students within the school
- Measure impact through pre/post surveys
- Make the game freely available for other schools to use
